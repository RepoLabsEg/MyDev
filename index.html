November 13th, 2024 — Sade Manor

CONFIDENTIAL

On the evening of November 13th, 2024, novelist Leila Sade hosted an intimate dinner at her secluded manor. Twelve guests were invited. Twelve chairs were set. The evening proceeded without incident until precisely 9:47 PM.

A blackout lasting exactly 60 seconds plunged the dining room into complete darkness. When the lights returned, a thirteenth chair had appeared at the table. Upon it rested a black envelope containing a single card:

"I sat here — yet none of you saw me."

No physical evidence of intrusion was found. No fingerprints. No footprints. No tampering with the electrical system. Yet every guest reported sensing something — a presence that existed only in the collective uncertainty of their memories.

THE TRUTH REVEALED

The Thirteenth Guest was never a person who entered the room.

It was a psychological presence — a collective construction of memory, doubt, and perception. When twelve people experienced a moment of uncertainty together, their minds filled the void with a presence that felt real.

The chair, the envelope, the message — all were placed by Leila Sade herself, the novelist who understood that the most powerful character is one that exists only in the minds of those who believe they witnessed something that was never truly there.

"We don't remember what happened. We remember what we believed happened."
